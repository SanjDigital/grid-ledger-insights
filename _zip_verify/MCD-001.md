## 2. The Three‑Tier Pricing Structure

| Tier | Rate | What It Represents |
|------|------|--------------------|
| **RBM Benchmark** | ~2.2% per month | Risk‑free policy rate |
| **Sector Typical MFI** | ~6% per month | Uncertainty premium for informal/SME lending |
| **EA‑001 (Faith Micro Finance)** | 10% per month | Additional opacity premium for an unverified node |

The gap between the benchmark and the sector typical rate is the **uncertainty premium** for informal enterprise lending. The gap between the sector typical rate and EA‑001 is the **opacity premium** for a specific unverified node.

## 3. The NEEF Signal

MWK 237 billion disbursed. MWK 206 billion in arrears. 87% non‑recovery.

What this proves:

- Scale alone does not create observability
- Capital deployment without verification does not solve productive uncertainty
- The problem is not "more lending" — capital cannot reliably observe what happened after deployment

---

## 4. Institutional Interpretation

> "Malawi's lending market is not suffering primarily from lack of capital. It is suffering from lack of observable productive reality. That is a measurement infrastructure problem."

The most important number in this briefing is not the MWK 70.7 billion loan book or the MWK 237 billion in NEEF disbursements. It is the 11.2% NPL ratio against a 5% regulatory benchmark. When an entire sector persistently breaches the regulatory impairment threshold, the question changes from "which lenders are disciplined?" to "what information is structurally missing from the underwriting environment?"
