# EA-004 — "MAGETSI AZIMA" OUTAGE LOG (NABIWI NODE)

**Source:** SMS production reports, Nabiwi-Chitsazo maize mill, M1 Corridor, Malawi
**Operator:** Production facility operator
**Period:** February 2024 – March 2026 (32-month observation window)
**Total Outage Messages:** 120+
**Classification:** Operator-generated, timestamped, contemporaneous infrastructure telemetry

## Summary
Power outages are not anomalies in the Nabiwi operating environment. They are the default
condition. The operator reported "Magetsi azima" (power is out), "Magets kulibe" (no power),
"Achita dimu" (it tripped), or equivalent terms over 120 times during the 32-month window.
Outages cluster during the rainy season, frequently extend across multiple days ("dzulo
mpaka pano" — yesterday until now), and are directly correlated with lost production,
lower remittances, and equipment stress.

## Key Finding
The 62-cycle adherence streak (93% operator adherence) and the 26-cycle clean subset
(100% cash-revenue alignment) were achieved DURING this chronic outage environment, not
in the absence of it. The Infrastructure Fragility Delta of 29.9 percentage points is a
direct measurement of the economic cost of these outages. The operator's discipline
survived what the grid did not.

## Methodology Note
This log was extracted from the SMS corpus that produced the 705 sealed cycles.
Outage reports are contemporaneous with production reporting and are independently
cross-referenceable against the ESCOM outage registry and the cycle log. Every
outage message can be mapped to a specific date and production window.

## Institutional Significance
This dataset answers the hardest question any DFI reviewer or lender will ask about
the 62-cycle adherence streak: "Were those 62 cycles a lucky window of grid stability,
or did the operator sustain discipline through chronic outages?" The answer is in the
data. The outages were chronic. The discipline held.
