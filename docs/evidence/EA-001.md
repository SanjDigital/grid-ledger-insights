## 1. The Document

**Lender:** Faith Micro Finance Limited  
**Location:** Lilongwe, Malawi  
**Date:** January 2025  
**Borrower:** Zaumoyo Farm (agro-processing operator)  
**Facility:** Working Capital  
**Amount:** MWK 1,200,000  
**Term:** Short Term  
**Interest Rate:** 10% per month (fixed)

---

## 2. What This Interest Rate Represents

10% per month = 120% per annum simple, or approximately 214% compounded annually.

This is not "expensive credit." This is undifferentiated risk pricing under infrastructure opacity.

The lender cannot see the borrower's production. The lender cannot separate operator discipline from grid instability. The lender cannot verify throughput between site visits. Therefore the lender prices for maximum uncertainty.

---

## 3. The Market Signal

This single-page offer letter is the empirical anchor for the GridLedger thesis. It proves that:

- The market already extends working capital to informal agro-processors
- The price of that capital is extraordinarily high
- The premium is driven by information asymmetry, not by intrinsic borrower risk
- A measurement infrastructure that reduces opacity can alter the pricing structure

---

## 4. The Counterfactual

If a Fragility Profile existed for this node — 62 clean cycles, 93% operator adherence, Infrastructure Fragility Delta quantified — a regulated institution could:

- Price the operator separately from the grid
- Offer working capital at a fraction of 10% per month
- Deploy capital against verified production cycles rather than against trust

The seal is the difference between 10% per month and a viable lending rate.
