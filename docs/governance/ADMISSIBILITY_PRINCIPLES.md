## 1. Evidentiary Capture Doctrine

Evidentiary capture does not require bad faith. It requires only that the verifier's independence cannot be structurally demonstrated. Ownership by any party with a financial interest in the verification output is sufficient to render that output contestable, regardless of the verifier's conduct.

---

## 2. Issuer-Pays Structure

The issuer-pays structure is an inherent feature of any certification system in which the evaluated entity funds the evaluation. This structure does not compromise admissibility where the verification methodology is independently replayable, frozen in versioned governance documents, and accessible to any auditor without restriction. The integrity of the verification output depends on architectural independence, not on the funding relationship.
